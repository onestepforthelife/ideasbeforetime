Key Findings

  • Market Structure: Global acrylic emulsion market valued at USD 12.3B (2024), growing at 7.1-7.7% CAGR through 2030
  • Polymer Hierarchy: Styrene Acrylics dominate (45.18% volume share), followed by VAM Acrylics (28%), Pure Acrylics (15-20%), and Acrylic Hybrids (5-10%)
  • India Growth: Acrylic Acid demand surging at 18.54% CAGR (53.55 KT in FY2021 ‚Üí 247.48 KT by FY2030), driven by SAP applications
  • Supply Constraints: India faces critical monomer import dependencies - Acrylic Acid (70-80%), Styrene (60-70%), VAM (100%)
  • Butyl Acrylate Shift: IOCL's 150 KT plant (July 2025) transforms market from 60 KT deficit to 80 KT surplus, eliminating imports
  • Margin Opportunity: Acrylic Hybrids offer highest margins (40-50%) with 65-70% import dependency = domestic manufacturing opportunity
Strategic Imperative: While new Butyl Acrylate capacity addresses supply quantity, persistent upstream monomer import dependencies (especially Acrylic Acid at 70-80%) maintain cost volatility. True competitiveness requires backward integration into monomer production.
